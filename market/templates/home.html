Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring System

Process of estimating the energy consumed by individual appliances given just a whole-house power meter reading

Identify the energy consumption of individual appliances given a whole-house power meter reading

About

A non-intrusive load monitoring (NILM) system, also called energy disaggregation systems, allows to obtain information relating to the power absorption of individual appliances. The first NILM system was proposed by George W. Hart in 1985. Load monitoring and identification is a method of determining electrical energy consumption of individual appliances based on the analysis of composite load measured from the main power meter in a building. They can supply information such as type of load, electricity consumption details of the appliances to both the consumer and the utility. This information can be used to formulate load plan strategies for optimal energy utilization.

Advantages

  • Appliance Load Monitoring (ALM) leads to the identification of high-energy consuming appliances.
  • Reduction in energy wastage can be achieved through monitoring energy consumption and relaying this information back to customers.
  • It helps the user to make a financial decision for when to use an appliance.
  • It helps the user to understand bills.
  • With the help NILM user could plan their monthly budget.
  • It can be used to formulate load plan strategies for optimal energy utilization.
Utility company

The utility company can estimate demand response potential in some locations from the fine information on the appliance levels.

Government

Governments spend billions of dollars on energy efficiency (EE) program every year. The fine load monitoring through NILM can help the government make wise decisions on EE policies.

Appliance company

Household electricity consumption information can prompt manufactures to speed up the study of high-performance equipment and guide the change of market of household appliance.

GENERAL FRAMEWORK OF NILM

A typical or general framework of NILM includes three steps to implement a practical NILM system

Data Acquisition

Data acquisition is a stage of obtaining aggregated load measurements from the household at a given time interval. During this stage, voltage and current measurements are obtained and processed to produce the power metrics. The steady-state or transient changes in the pre-processed signals, corresponds to the operation of individual appliances.
Appliance identification can be made by using an algorithm and comparing the energy consumption of each appliance with known features of a typical appliances in the data.
Load identification refers to learning and inference stage of NILM system by means of supervised and unsupervised, or semi-supervised learning approaches. The ultimate goal of NILM system is to identify and disaggregate load with unsupervised learning.

REDD

Reference Energy Disaggregation Data Set

REDD dataset used for training the model

Visit website
REDD, a data set for energy disaggregation. The data contains power consumption from real homes, for the whole house as well as for each individual circuit in the house. The dataset directory contains average power readings for both the two power mains and the individual circuits of the house. The main directory consists of several 6 houses directories, each of which contain all the power readings for a single house. Each house subdirectory consists of a labels.dat and several channels_i.dat files. The labels file contains channel numbers and a text label indicating the general category of device on this channel.
